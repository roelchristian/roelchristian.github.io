Hi, I’m Roel. Welcome to my homepage! I’m a twenty-something dilettante from Manila who is interested in languages, maps, and typography. I also write, and this website is home to my (mostly incoherent) ramblings.

I started this site in 2020 as a new place to self-host my blog, which by then had been running for some four or five years. Over time, however, the site grew to include bits and pieces of my other side projects.

I have been writing for as long as I remember, although I’d normally shy away from calling myself a “writer”—or from calling myself by any other label, for that matter, which is why I always struggle more than what is necessary to write a single paragraph introducing myself. (I prefer to call myself an “observer,” but I realize that that sounds a lot more conceited than “writer” ever is.)

Besides writing, I also enjoy learning languages and learning about languages. I learned Spanish in high school and could now comfortably speak it, in addition to four others: my native Filipino; English, which most everyone here learns at schools; and French and Portuguese, which I had studied later on. With some effort I could also speak and understand Italian and German. I tried learning Russian while in college, but would always give up after just a couple of weeks. You can also find documentation about my conlang Iridian here or check the Latex files for its grammar (whose latest version I had started working on in late 2019) here.
