Vowels

Iridian has six phonemic vowels, as listed in the table below.

Front Central Back
Close i ⟨и⟩ ɨ ⟨ы⟩ u ⟨у⟩
Mid e ⟨е⟩ o ⟨о⟩
Open a ⟨а⟩

In addition, diphthongs can be formed by the semivowels й /j/ and ў /w/, as follows:

-й -ў
а ай /aj/ аў /aw/
е ей /ej/ –
и іи /ij/ –
о ой /oj/ оў /ow/
у уй /uj/ --

Unstressed /a e o/ are raised to [ɐ ɯ ɔ] respectively; in unstressed word-final position, only the latter set appears. Thus we have

мамка [ˈmamkɐ] ‘mother’
пяшице [ˈpʲaɕt͡ɕɪt͡sɯ] ‘(he) ate (conjunctive)’
горе [ˈɣorɯ] ‘hill (patientive)’

In more extreme cases, especially in colloquial speech, unstressed vowels can sometimes be further reduced to [ə] or even be elided entirely. This reduction often causes the lengthening of the preceding consonant or in the cases of plosive, the aspiration of the preceding plosive.

мамка [ˈmamkə] or [ˈmamkʰ] ‘mother’
горе [ˈɣɔrə] or [ˈɣɔrː] ‘hill (patientive)’

The central vowel /ɨ/ written as ы is diphthongized to [œj] (as in French oeil) in all positions. This sound has also merged with the diphthong /ij/.

Ирцомы [ˈɪrˈt͡somœj] ‘Iridia (gen.)’
прѣсты [ˈprʲɛstœj] ‘enemy (gen.)’
зушталніи ють [zuˈʃtaɫnœj juc] ‘the happy boy’

The remaining -й glides have also further simplified. Both /aj/ and /ej/ have merged into /ej/, while /uj/ and /oj/ have merged into /oj/. The former pair is realized as [ɐj] while the latter pair is realized as [uj].

Aлексей [ɐˈlɛksɐj] ‘Alexey’
лейте [ˈlɐjtɯ] ‘room’
лайка [ˈlɐjkɐ] ‘foot’

Plosives

The initial velar stop /k/ is affricated as [kx] while initial /g/ is realized as [ɣ]. Both are lenited to [x] and [ɣ] respectively in intervocalic position. At the end of a word, /k/ and /g/ merge to [x].

акузаце [ɐxuˈzat͡sɯ] ‘accusation’
грум [ɣrum] ‘shout’
витак [ˈvitɐx] ‘road’
