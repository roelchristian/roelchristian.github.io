Vowels

Oral Vowels

Iridian has six pairs of corresponding long and short vowels. With the exception of /a aː/ and /u uː/, long vowels are tenser than their short counterparts. In addition standard Iridian also features the high central vowel [ɨ] as an allophone of /ɛ/ and /ɪ/ and the low central [ɐ] as an allophone of /a/, in unstressed positions.
Table 1.1. Vowel inventory of Standard Iridian.
Close ɪ iː ʏ yː (ɨ) u uː
Mid ɛ eː ɔoː
Open (ɐ) a aː

Consonants

Stops

Initial stops are affricated when following a pause, so that the velar pair/k g/ are realized as [>kx>gG], the palatal pair [c J] as [>cç>éJ]1and the dentalpair /t d/ as [>t”T>d”D] (the last pair is written without the diacritics in theexamples). This sound change can be traced to the initial aspirated stops*kh, *gh, *thand *dhin Old Iridian weakening to affricates. The labial stops/p b/ are unaffected by this process as most instances of *phand *bhhavemerged to [b] or [v] in modern Iridian
